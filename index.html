What we do

Cakes

Whole fruit cakes, Victoria sponges, and lemon drizzles baked fresh each morning. We slice to order and box them in greaseproof paper, not Perspex domes.

  • Baked fresh before 7am
  • Sliced while you wait
  • Recipes unchanged for decades

Biscuits

Custard creams, ginger nuts, shortbread, and bourbons from the same cutters we've used for years. They crack properly in the teeth and leave crumbs on the tablecloth.

  • Baked in batches throughout the day
  • Sold loose by weight or number
  • Proper butter, not vegetable oil

Questions we've answered a thousand times

What time do you bake the fresh cakes?

Everything's out of the oven by 7am, six days a week. Sunday we open later with whatever's left from Saturday.

Can I order a birthday cake with specific writing on it?

Yes. Three days' notice, and we'll pipe whatever you need. We've done everything from 'Happy 90th Doris' to 'Sorry About Your Cat'.

Do you do gluten-free or vegan options?

Not as a regular thing. Our flour supplier doesn't do the substitute lines, and we won't sell what we can't bake properly.

How long do your biscuits stay fresh?

In the tin, a week. In our paper bags on the counter, three days. In your mouth, about thirty seconds.

Can I buy sweets by the specific weight?

We do quarter-pound, half-pound, or full-pound paper bags. You point at what you want behind the glass, we weigh it while you watch.

Do you deliver cake orders?

Collection only. The cakes travel better that way, and it keeps the price where it should be.

What's the oldest recipe you still use?

The rich fruit cake. We've got the handwritten card from 1974, stains and all. Still measures in ounces.

Are you open on bank holidays?

Usually closed. If we're ever open, it'll be on the door and our answerphone the week before.
